# CEFR Framework

## Purpose

Explains the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR) and how Babblr implements it.

## CEFR Levels

### A1 - Beginner
**Can**: Introduce self, ask/answer simple questions, interact simply

**Vocabulary**: ~500-1000 words

**Grammar**: Present tense, basic sentence structure

**Example**: "Hello, my name is Maria. I am from Spain. I like coffee."

### A2 - Elementary
**Can**: Describe family, shopping, work, immediate needs

**Vocabulary**: ~1000-2000 words

**Grammar**: Past tense, basic future, comparatives

**Example**: "Yesterday I went to the market. I bought apples and bread."

### B1 - Intermediate
**Can**: Handle most travel situations, describe experiences, express opinions

**Vocabulary**: ~2000-3000 words

**Grammar**: All major tenses, conditional, subjunctive

**Example**: "If I had more time, I would travel to South America. I've always wanted to visit Peru."

### B2 - Upper Intermediate
**Can**: Understand complex texts, interact fluently with natives, produce detailed text

**Vocabulary**: ~3000-4000 words

**Grammar**: All tenses, complex structures

### C1 - Advanced
**Can**: Understand demanding texts, express fluently and spontaneously, use language flexibly

**Vocabulary**: ~4000-6000 words

### C2 - Mastery
**Can**: Understand virtually everything, express with precision, near-native fluency

**Vocabulary**: ~6000+ words

## How Babblr Uses CEFR

- **Conversation Difficulty**: Vocabulary and grammar matched to level
- **Assessments**: Placement tests determine CEFR level
- **Progress Tracking**: Students advance through levels
